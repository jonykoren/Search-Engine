MacKenzie Scott Bezos [3] ( née Tuttle ; April 7, 1970) is an American novelist and philanthropist . She is the former wife of Amazon founder Jeff Bezos , to whom she was married from 1993 until their divorce in 2019. [4] In 2014, she founded the anti-bullying organization Bystander Revolution , where she serves as executive director.

In 1993, Mackenzie married Jeff Bezos , who later became the founder and CEO of Amazon , making them the richest couple in the world. They announced their intent to divorce in January 2019. [5] In April 2019, Bezos announced on Twitter that she had reached a $35 billion divorce settlement, making her the third richest woman in the world . [6] In May 2019, Bezos signed on the Giving Pledge initiative to donate at least half of her wealth to charitable causes. [7]

Early life [ edit ]

MacKenzie S. Tuttle was born on April 7, 1970, in San Francisco, California , to a financial planner father and a homemaker mother. [8] In 1988, she graduated from Hotchkiss School in Connecticut. [9]

Tuttle earned her bachelor's degree in English at Princeton University with highest honors in 1992. [1] She studied under writer Toni Morrison , who said Tuttle was "one of the best students I've ever had in my creative writing classes." [8]

Personal life [ edit ]

In 1992, MacKenzie worked as a research associate for Jeff Bezos at D. E. Shaw , a New York City hedge fund . [10] They married in 1993, [11] and moved to Seattle in 1994. They have four children: three sons, and one daughter adopted from China. [12]

In 2014, MacKenzie founded Bystander Revolution , an anti-bullying organization, where she serves as executive director. [13]

On January 9, 2019, MacKenzie and Jeff Bezos announced via Twitter that they would divorce. [5] On April 4, 2019, the divorce was finalized, with Jeff Bezos keeping 75% of the couple's Amazon stock and MacKenzie getting $35.6 billion in Amazon stock. Mackenzie also declared she would keep the Bezos last name rather than revert to her maiden name. [14] She is the third richest woman in the world, as well as one of the wealthiest people overall. [15]

Awards [ edit ]

  • She is the author of The Testing of Luther Albright , a debut novel, which in 2006 won an American Book Award . [16]
